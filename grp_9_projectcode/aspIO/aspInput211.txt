

Sailors End Long  Trip

	(SOUTH SEA ISLANDS, August 1, 1947) Six sailors finished a 5,000-mile sailboat trip yesterday.@@@@The men crossed the sea just like people did long ago.@@@@Their boat did not have an engine.@@@@All they had were oars.@@@@The men left Peru on April 28.@@@@Sailors wanted to show how, in the past, people traveled miles across the seas.@@@@For supplies, Sailors took food and water.@@@@Sailors cooked fish on a small stove.@@@@For company, Sailors took along a parrot.@@@@The captain's name is Thor.@@@@Since Thor landed, he has told many tales.@@@@Often, Thor and his crew ran into storms.@@@@One time, someone fell overboard.@@@@Sailors quickly saved someone with ropes.@@@@For months Sailors did not see land.@@@@At last, Sailors spotted some birds.@@@@But more weeks went by without land.@@@@On day 101 of the trip, Sailors reached the shores of the South Sea Islands.@@@@The name of their boat is the Kon Tiki.@@@@Kon Tiki traveled 4,950 miles.@@@@But the men don't plan to sail it back to Peru.@@@@Sailors will take a ship home.@@@@Sailors are going to let someone else do all that hard work.@@@@1#  Who is the captain of the sailboat?@@@@2#  What is the name of the boat?@@@@3#  When did Sailors reach the shore?@@@@4#  Where did the trip start?@@@@5#  Why did Sailors make the trip?
